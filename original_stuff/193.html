Prayers

Treasure in the jars of clay
"But we have this treasure in jars of clayT)" data-cr="#cen-NIV-28867T" style="box-sizing: border-box; position: relative; vertical-align: top; top: 0px;"> to show that this all-surpassing power is from GodU)" data-cr="#cen-NIV-28867U" style="box-sizing: border-box; position: relative; vertical-align: top; top: 0px;"> and not from us." 2 Corinthians 4: 7
Paul in his letter to Corinthians presents himself as a Jar of clay. He is of the opinion that it is the best way to explain who a Christian is. But he says even though the jar is made of clay but still God kept a treasure in this. In the first century context many people kept their treasures, especially their coins in a clay jars and kept inside the ground so that they can keep their treasure safely from Wars and robberies. In that context Paul explained this great spiritual thought to explain who a Christian is. When we study this concept I think three concepts are to be discussed
Creativity
A clay jar is always a creation of a creator. Every potter who makes a vessel has an intention when he makes it. So for that purpose he/she designs that vessel. So every vessel has a purpose that the creator intended for that Jar. In Jeremiah chapter 18 we read about a potter who moulds the clay according to his will and wish for a particular purpose. Paul is saying every Christian is a jar of clay which was moulded by our creator, the almighty God. When he designed us like this He has a special purpose in us. We live in this world to fulfill that purpose.
Fragility
The second word that we need to remember when we think about the the jars of clay is fragility. Human is a fragile being and God has taken a risk to keep the treasure in that fragile jar. Just like any other clay jar every human us prone to many destructing experiences in life. But Paul explains that in verse 8 and 9; "We are hard pressed on every side,V)" data-cr="#cen-NIV-28868V" style="box-sizing: border-box; position: relative; vertical-align: top; top: 0px;"> but not crushed; perplexed,W)" data-cr="#cen-NIV-28868W" style="box-sizing: border-box; position: relative; vertical-align: top; top: 0px;"> but not in despair; persecuted,X)" data-cr="#cen-NIV-28869X" style="box-sizing: border-box; position: relative; vertical-align: top; top: 0px;"> but not abandoned;Y)" data-cr="#cen-NIV-28869Y" style="box-sizing: border-box; position: relative; vertical-align: top; top: 0px;"> struck down, but not destroyed". But those who have this treasure at our clay jars we will have the assurance that we will not be destroyed even though we face all kinds of difficulties in our lives
Humility
This word reminds us about another aspect when we think about jars of clay. God came down into this form of clay jars to identify with everyone one. Philippians chapter 2 "though he was in the form of God, did not count equality with God, He made himself nothing by taking the very nature of a servant being made in human likeness being found in appearance as a man,he humbled himself by becoming obedient to death even death on a cross!" This clay jars reminds us about the humbleness of Jesus and challenges us to follow the life style of humility. We are once again on the Advent season. Christmas is always reminding us about this humility where Jesus became human to redeem this whole world. When we celebrate Christmas this year let us remember that God became a jar clay to bring this Gospel to us.
God has placed this treasure, the gospel in our clay jars. So we are the clay jars and we need to remember who is our creator and what is purpose in this world. We must understand how fragile we are but still under the protection of God almighty our creator. And also this clay jars reminds us about the about the humility that we need to have in our lives to imitate the life style of Jesus. God wants to place this treasure in our clay Jars. But are we ready to carry it in our Iives.

I wish you all a Happy Christmas and a prosperous new year.

V. M. Mathew achen
Youth Chaplain
