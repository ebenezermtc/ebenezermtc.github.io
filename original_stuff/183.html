Tower of Babel – the misuse of human brain

 

    

 

Tower of Babel is a beautiful image that Bible portrays to us to understand that how the human brain could be used in a disastrous way. As our children are going to begin a new academic year, I think this image has to be a warning to tell them the importance of the proper use of human brain. Human brain and intelligence are the beautiful creations of God and a priceless gift to human beings. God created these for the benefit of human beings and to provide provisions for the common living.

Tower of Babel is not all a negative story but it tells a few positives that can help everyone.  It talks about the unity of human beings, it talks about human unity to have a common goal, it talks about the power of language for the unification of the people and it also talks about human intelligence to plan something new and huge. But the three intensions that prompted the people to have this huge tower tell us how the human brain is used for a disastrous goal. Bible says, in Genesis chapter 11, people had three intensions behind this huge endeavour. Not to be scattered, it should reach the skies/heavens and to have a name. These intensions are not so dangerous at the first glance but these intensions are purely selfish and it wanted to challenge the creator.

One of the beautiful gifts that God provided to human beings is the freedom of choice. But if that freedom is not used properly it might be the worst thing that can happen in a life. In this story of the tower, people used this freedom not for the common benefit but only to meet their selfish interest. God has provided us this freedom to use to live and to live together. Selfishness has nothing to do with it and when selfishness directs it, it will end up in trouble.

We are all created by a supreme being and the creator has a purpose when he created everything. But when the creation uses everything to negate the supremacy of the creator then the creation will be in astray. EGO is edging God out. And that is what happened in the tower of Babel. The people negated the supremacy of God and wanted to reach out to God by using their brain and abilities. When people think we can do everything of our own then we will disown what is above us and what is to be depended on

But the story ends by telling us that whatever human make could be destroyed in a minute. Some of us think we can attain anything and construct anything that we want but if God is the author and almighty then all these things could be vanished if he decides.

God has given us the intelligence and brain but these are to be used by using the heavenly wisdom otherwise it will end up in problems. God provides us every opportunity to learn and to grow but when we think we can depend on ourselves and not on God then all our achievements will be with selfish motives. Let us trust in Him, we are blessed but that is not to edge God out but to depend on Him.

 

V. M. Mathew achen

Youth Chaplain
