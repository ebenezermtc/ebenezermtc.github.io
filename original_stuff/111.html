VICARS MESSAGE-    FROM INVOLVEMENT TO COMMITMENT

Many of the important human values seen to began with the alphabet ‘C’ – compassion, charity, care, concern, companionship, choice, courage,  creativity, charisma, candor, communion, conscious, and there is great word commitment!  Of course the word of all words ‘Christ’ begin with C and so also important word like church, Christian, Christmas, community, conscience, and communication.  Commitment is a value or a way of life that can bring us happiness and wellbeing. After fifty years of married life an elderly couple were asked to express their feelings and the man replied, “It was difficult but worth it.”This is commitment, difficult but in the long run worth it!  

In March 2011 Japan was hit by a massive earthquake followed by a killer tsunami and thousands lost their lives and homes and everything they owned.  But in the midst of this great tragedy we witnessed true stories of great courage and commitment.  People received the warning through the city speakers that are set up outside near the sea shore.  They heard, ‘A big tsunami is coming. You need to evacuate.’ Shortly after, they heard, ‘Run!’ The announcement suddenly went off the air and people never heard from the speakers again, or from the people who made the announcement.  The few people assigned to announce it were at the disaster prevention center, and they went missing.  They sacrificed their lives to send everyone else to a safer place.  They gave priority to others safety.  The disaster prevention center is now buried under the mud and sand brought on by the tsunami.  There were also a hand full of heroes who remained at the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear power plant, knowing that they were risking their own lives, but trying to bring the plant under control and prevent a wider environmental and public health catastrophe.  This is the recent manifestation of commitment and dedication to the point of death.

The dictionary defines commitment as a pledge to do.  It also says commitment is the state of being emotionally or intellectually bound to a cause or a person.  It is said that life is meaningful and joyful when there is a cause for which we live and for which we are ready to die.  Happiness and wellbeing depend on two important factors, namely, meaningful life and loving relationships.  We create meaning by living and dying for a cause.  A cause possesses you!  Most of those who lived strongly for a cause have been killed or martyred. The first great example is Jesus Christ.  He lived for a cause and gave his life for that cause.  He was focused and he knew what the purpose of his existence was and he willingly died for that cause.  He lived and died for one reason, namely to reveal that our God  is a God of love and that He loves us and love is the ultimate purpose of life.  He challenged people to give up selfishness, violence and hatred.  Love is explained as a choice and commitment.  We choose to love a certain person or a cause and we are committed to that person or a cause, to the point of death.  This the reason why the high point of every Christian wedding is a vow the couples solemnly proclaim in public view- that in good times and bad times, in sickness and health and in poverty or in wealth they will stand by each other.

The commitment results in interior joy and inner peace.  True commitment is the fruit of love and justice.  Commitment calls us to move beyond just being involved in a project, job or even a relationship; it needs disciplined dedication, being focused and hard work.

MAY THE GOOD LORD BLESS US ALL.                                                   SUJAN ACHEN
