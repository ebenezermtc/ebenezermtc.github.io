Step out of your comfort zone

 Dearly Beloved in Christ,

 God is a God of surprise.  He called many people to leave their comfort zone.  It started with Noah.  God commanded Noah to build an ark and to be ready to set out during the great floods to come.  It must have sounded like a great big crazy idea to Noah and his family but Noah obeyed.  Abram was a good man and was living a very peaceful life looking after his cattle and sheep and suddenly God called him and told to leave his comfortable place, “Go out of your land, your family and your father’s house, to the land I will show you”.  God changed his name also and he became Abraham.  Moses was looking after the sheep of his father-in-law.  God called him and asked to leave his comfort zone and go back to set his people free. The New Testament is full of such calling. Peter, Andrew, John, Mathew…….Jesus called his apostles to leave their comfort zones to follow Him.  God touched Paul and transformed him.  Every great conversion is a story of leaving one’s comfort zone and launching in to the unknown.  Mother Teresa was leading quite an easy life in her convent in Calcutta.  But God made her leave her comfort zone and challenged her to go to the streets of Calcutta.  The rest is what we say history.

Leaving your comfort zone means doing something you are normally not comfortable with the intention of beating the status quo and conquering your fears.  Our comfort zone could be simple things like a fixed place at home, in the office, or in the Church and if someone else occupies that chair we get annoyed.  Children will struggle to change their school, though it is good for them.  This is because they are used to their old school and their friends.  Young people may not easily change the group to which they belong because it is not easy to leave their comfort zone.  Bad habits or unwanted and negative relationships can slowly become our comfort zone.  We need the courage to step out of our comfort zone and seek for newness and more positive attitudes.

        The possibilities are endless, but for most of us remain undiscovered.  It is always difficult to step out of our comfort zone but it is worth to us.  May the Good Lord help us to step out of our comfort zone.

                                                                                                              In His Service

                                                                                                     SUJAN ACHEN
