Vicar’s Message                  Examine Your Priorities

Dearly beloved in Christ,

            Knowledge is weightless, a treasure you can always carry easily. Wisdom is defined as a deep understanding of people, things, events or situations resulting in the ability to choose or act to consistently produce the optimum results with a minimum of time and energy. Wisdom is comprehension of what is time or right coupled with optimum judgment for action. One important aspect of wisdom is the ability to choose between what is really important and which is secondary to lead a good and happy life. Wisdom allows us to distinguish between that which should be placed at the center of our lives and those things that have to be kept at the periphery of our life. Happiness and wellbeing depends on our capacity to give priority to that which is really important, let us call it core elements for our wellbeing and happiness. This is yet another choice we constantly make in our lives. This is a 30second speech attributed to Bryan Dyson (CEO of Coco Cola). Imagine life as a game in which you are juggling some five balls in the air. You name them- work, family, health, friends and spirit and you are keeping all of these in the air you will soon understand that work is a rubber ball. If you drop it, it will bounce back. But the other four balls family, health, friend, and spirit are made of glass. If you drop one of these: they will be irrevocable scuffed, marked, nicked, damaged or even shattered. They will never be the same. You must understand that and strive for it. All of us at a certain point in our life have to examine our life to see if we are focused on the core issues. We end up by being unhappy at the end of the day because of our incapacity to prioritize and hence we more towards a stress filled life and it finally leads to frustration, pain and depression.

         Jesus is setting a priority. He has not come to their house to have a good meal but to build up a relationship. God set his own priorities for us. He does not want sacrifices of animals, incense and a new moon festivals, but instead he asks us to be more humble, gentle, compassionate, just merciful and honest.(Mic:6:8) It is important that we stop and reflect about our own priorities in life. What is the driving force of our life? Is it money, power, luxury, pleasures of life, gadgets, material things or are we living our life for greater purposes- God, spirituality, family, friends, good health and love. Let us set our hearts on things that will bring us real happiness and let us be aware of our priorities in life. Value has a value only if its value is valued.

            All our students are going to schools and colleges from this month for the new academic year. I wish them all the best and good luck.

May God Bless us All                               SUJAN ACHEN
