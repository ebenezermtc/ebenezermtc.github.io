KNOWLEDGE OF GOD

Beloved brothers and sisters in Christ,

We are living in a fast paced world undated with information and have been driven to unreasonable limits.  Material prosperity, success, and creative strength have not made life meaning full, even if they were filled to capacity.  Currently, people live in relationships devoid of love’s commitment or moral fidelity.  In the midst of bright urban lights, there is demand for everything, except morality with the loss of God.  This leads to loss of joy, love, light, and peace.  There is a complete sense of alienation in the world.  The farcical and the absurd are the hallmarks of our trapped society, devoid of all hope.  Solomon, who knew more and was renowned more than anybody else in his time, asserted the pointlessness of existence.  His book Ecclesiastes opens with the words “Vanity of Vanities! All is Vanity.”

            Human can relate to the material world, to the world of knowledge, and machines only up to a point.  If he does not rise above that, every association in his life is reduced to that level and becomes an object for his own purpose.  Pascal said, ‘”there is a God- shaped vacuum in the heart of every man, and only God can fill it.”  The three virtues that are missing among the people include lack of faithfulness and honesty in daily life; lack of kindness in personal relationships; and lack of knowledge of God.  In Hosea: 4:8 the priests rejoice in the sins of the people, “They lap it up and lick their lips for more.”

             The people have no knowledge of God or His law and therefore, they are unfaithful to Him and deceitful in their dealings with one another.  Their wickedness is the reason for the drought they are suffering.  Nature suffers for Human’s sin, but God loves His people in spite of their sins and rebellion.  He still desires the people’s repentance rather than their destruction.  Departure from God denudes humans, and results in the death of meaning in spite of all the learning, education, and hyperactivity of modern man.  T. S. Eliot gave a powerful summary of such situation, written as “Where is the life that we have lost in living? Where is the wisdom we have lost in knowledge? Where is the knowledge we have lost in information? The cycle of heaven in twenty centuries bring us farther from God and nearer to the Dust.”  If we truly see God with all our heart, will we find Him?  Knowledge has a very practical purpose, when we come to know the truth; we grow in that truth through learning more of God and His ways.  Knowing God is to know God’s Word.

                                                                    Yours in His ministry,

Sujan achen
